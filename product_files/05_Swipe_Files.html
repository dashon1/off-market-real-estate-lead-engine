DIRECT MAIL TEMPLATES

Physical mail has the highest response rates for motivated sellers. Use these for your primary outreach.

Letter #1: Tax Deed Introduction

Direct Mail
Best For: Tax delinquent properties Length: ~250 words Timing: First touch, Day 1

Dear [Owner Name],

I'm reaching out because I noticed the property at [Property Address] may have some back taxes owed. I know these situations can feel overwhelming, and I wanted to offer a potential solution.

I work with homeowners who find themselves in this position. Rather than waiting for a tax sale, we can often help you sell the property directly and use the proceeds to clear the debt while putting money back in your pocket.

Here's what I can offer:

  • A fast, no-hassle sale — no repairs needed
  • I handle all the paperwork and closing costs
  • You get to choose your timeline
  • No agent commissions or listing fees

If you're facing back taxes on this property, I'd like to hear your situation. Maybe there's a way we can help each other.

Please reply to this letter or call me directly at [Your Phone]. I'm happy to chat about your options, no pressure.

Respectfully,

[Your Name]
[Your Company]

Customization Tips: Mention the specific county if you know it. Add the approximate tax amount if you have it. Keep the tone empathetic — they may be embarrassed about the situation.

Letter #4: Follow-Up (Non-Response)

Direct Mail
Best For: Follow-up after no response to first letter Length: ~200 words Timing: Day 10-14 (second touch)

Dear [Owner Name],

I sent you a letter recently about your property at [Property Address]. I'm following up because I didn't hear back and wanted to make sure you received it.

I'm still interested in helping if you're considering selling. I understand life gets busy and letters can get lost in the mail.

If you're already working with someone else or have decided to keep the property, that's completely fine — just wanted to make sure you had the option.

Compliance Note: Keep follow-up messages shorter and less aggressive. You're reminding, not pushing. Over-contacting can damage your reputation and potentially create legal issues.

SMS/TEXT MESSAGE TEMPLATES

Texts have 98% open rates but require careful wording and compliance. Keep messages short and personal.

Text #1: Initial Contact - Tax Property

SMS
Best For: Tax deed leads Length: 160 characters Timing: Day 7 text (after mail)

Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I saw your property on [Street Name] in [City] may have some tax issues. I help homeowners in your situation sell fast without the hassle. Are you interested in hearing your options? Reply Y to chat.

SMS
Best For: Second text touch Length: ~140 characters Timing: Day 14 if no response

[First Name], I sent you a letter about your property on [Street]. Wanted to make sure you saw it. If you're thinking about selling, I'd love to hear from you. - [Your Name], [Phone]

Text #3: Interest Check

SMS
Best For: Probable interest leads Length: ~150 characters Timing: After positive response

Thanks for responding, [First Name]! I can help with [Property Address]. I buy houses in any condition and close fast. Can I call you today to discuss? What time works best? - [Your Name]

Text #4: Quick Value Prop

SMS
Best For: Quick hook message Length: ~130 characters Timing: For engaged leads

[First Name], I help homeowners sell fast for cash. No repairs, no fees, close in 2 weeks. Do you want a free offer on your property? Reply Y to learn more.

Text Compliance Reminders:

  • Only text people who have given implied consent (their info is public record)
  • Include opt-out instructions ("Reply STOP to opt out")
  • Honor opt-out requests immediately
  • Check state-specific TCPA requirements
  • Never use automated text sends without compliance verification

COLD CALL SCRIPTS

Use these for live calls when you connect. Focus on listening more than talking.

Script #1: Basic Introduction

Cold Call
Use: When person answers Length: 2-3 minutes

Opening:

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from [Company]. I'm reaching out about your property at [Address]. Do you have a quick moment to chat?"

If Yes:

"Great, thanks. So I work with homeowners who want to sell their property quickly. I saw [Address] come up in my research and wanted to check if you're considering a sale."

Qualifying Questions:

  • "Can you tell me a little about your situation?"
  • "What's your timeline looking like?"
  • "Are there any repairs or issues I should know about?"

Closer:

"Based on what you've shared, I'd love to take a look at the property and put together an offer. Would you be open to a quick walkthrough this week?"

If No / Busy:

"No problem at all. When would be a better time to call? Or if it's easier, you can reach me at [Your Phone] — I can send you some info."

Script #2: Handling Pushback

Cold Call
Use: When person is hesitant

Common Objections & Responses:

"I'm not interested."

"I completely understand, and I appreciate your time. If you change your mind, my number is [Your Phone]. Have a great day."

"I'm already working with an agent."

"That's great. Is it selling? Sometimes properties sit on the market for a while, and I can help if they need to move faster. No pressure though."

"What's your offer?"

"I'd need to see the property first to give you an accurate number. But based on [condition/location], I'd estimate somewhere in the [range] — would that be worth discussing?"

"I don't have time for this."

"I completely understand. This is a big decision. Would it help if I sent you some information so you can review it when you have a moment? You can look it over and call me back if you're interested."

OUTREACH COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

Before Launching Any Outreach Campaign:

Federal (TCPA):

  • [ ] Verify you're not contacting Do Not Call Registry numbers
  • [ ] Have a system to honor opt-out requests immediately
  • [ ] Include identification in all messages (your name, company)
  • [ ] Never use automated dialing for live answering without proper consent

State-Specific:

  • [ ] Research your state's specific real estate communication laws
  • [ ] Some states require specific language in marketing materials
  • [ ] Florida, Texas, and California have specific rules for real estate outreach

Best Practices:

  • [ ] Document your outreach methods and timing
  • [ ] Keep records of all contacts and responses
  • [ ] Use a compliance-focused calling/texting platform
  • [ ] Consult with a real estate attorney if unsure

Use these templates as starting points. Always customize for your market, test different approaches, and track results. What works best in your area may differ from others.
